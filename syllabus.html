How to Share a Google Drive Link Publicly

Watch the video guide & follow the steps below

▼

▶ Video: How to Share a Google Drive File Publicly (Step-by-Step)

📋 Step-by-Step Instructions
1
Open Google Drive Go to drive.google.com and sign in with your Google account.
2
Locate your file Find the PDF, document, or file you want to share with students.
3
Right-click → Share Right-click the file and select Share, or click the file once and press the Share button (🔗) in the toolbar.
4
Change access to "Anyone with the link" In the sharing dialog, click Change to anyone with the link under General Access. Make sure the role is set to Viewer.
5
Copy the link Click Copy link — the shareable URL is now copied to your clipboard.
6
Paste it in the form above Paste the copied link into the Google Drive Link field in the upload form and submit.
⚠️
Important: If you skip Step 4 and leave the file as Restricted, students will see a "You need access" error when they try to open the link. Always set access to Anyone with the link before uploading.
💡
Pro tip: After copying the link, you can test it by opening a new incognito/private browser window and pasting the URL — if the file opens without signing in, it's correctly set to public.
